## 🔱 Navy NUPOC Program - Complete Guide

### What is NUPOC?

The **Nuclear Propulsion Officer Candidate (NUPOC)** program is one of the Navy's most elite commissioning paths. It offers a direct route for college students and recent graduates to become nuclear-trained officers serving on submarines or aircraft carriers—with **full pay while still in school**, no ROTC required, and no prior military experience needed.

This is a serious alternative to civilian engineering careers, offering:
- Immediate income ($60K+/year while still a student)
- World-class technical training
- Leadership responsibility from day one
- Exceptional post-Navy career opportunities
- Clear 5-year commitment with defined exit

| **Age** | ✅ Under 29 at commissioning | Must be 19-29 at commissioning (waivers to 31 possible) |

1. Grades in technical courses (math, physics, engineering)

His QUEST program, leadership experience, and BAE Systems background all strengthen his candidacy.

### Three NUPOC Career Tracks

#### 1. 🚢 Submarine Officer (Designator 1170)
- Immediate entry to nuclear training after OCS
- Serve on nuclear-powered attack submarines or ballistic missile submarines
- Lead reactor plant operations and tactical systems
- Qualify for "Dolphins" (submarine warfare insignia)
- **Most competitive track** - values technical excellence + leadership

#### 2. ⚓ Surface Warfare Officer - Nuclear (Designator 1160)
- First tour on non-nuclear surface ship (earn SWO qualification)
- Then enter nuclear training pipeline
- Serve on nuclear aircraft carriers (Nimitz/Ford-class)
- Oversee carrier reactor systems
- **Good for those who prefer larger ships/more crew interaction**

#### 3. 🔬 Naval Reactors Engineer (Designator 100B)
- **No sea duty** - shore-based technical role
- Work at Naval Reactors HQ or prototype facilities
- Design reviews, compliance, reactor oversight
- Requires higher GPA (~3.7+) - likely not Carter's path
- **Best for pure technical focus without operational leadership**

**Recommendation for Carter:** Submarine or Surface Nuclear tracks align best with his GPA, leadership experience, and hands-on engineering background.

### The NUPOC Interview: What to Expect

The interview happens at **Naval Reactors Headquarters in Washington, DC** (convenient for a UMD student!). It's a multi-day evaluation:

**Day 1: Administrative & Security**
- Document verification
- Security clearance paperwork

**Day 2: Pre-Interviews**
- Interview with escort officer
- Interview with Director of Nuclear Accessions
- Preparation for technical interviews

**Day 3: Technical Interviews + Admiral's Interview**
- **2 technical interviews** (submarines/surface) or 3 (for NRE track)
  - Heavy focus on calculus, physics, and engineering fundamentals
  - They will ask about coursework on your transcript
  - Problem-solving under pressure
- **Admiral's Interview** (final)
  - Character, motivation, leadership
  - Why nuclear? Why Navy?

**How to Prepare:**
1. **Review fundamentals:**
   - Calculus (derivatives, integrals, differential equations)
   - Physics (mechanics, E&M, thermodynamics)
   - Basic engineering (statics, dynamics, materials)
   
2. **Practice explaining concepts** - they test if you truly understand, not just memorize

3. **Prepare your "why":**
   - Why nuclear Navy specifically?
   - What draws you to submarines/surface ships?
   - How has your leadership prepared you?

4. **Get the NUPOC Study Guide** from your recruiter

5. **Be yourself** - they're evaluating character as much as knowledge
