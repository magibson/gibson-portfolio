## Areas to Strengthen

### ⚠️ Honest Assessment

| Gap | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----|--------|------------|
| **3.1 GPA** | Below typical competitive threshold (3.3+ for automotive, 3.5+ for consulting). Won't disqualify him but reduces filter-through rates on online applications. | Lead with QUEST program, emphasize hands-on experience over grades. Some companies (defense, manufacturing) care less about GPA. |
| **IT Internship vs. ME Internship** | BAE role was hardware asset management/IT, not mechanical engineering design work | Reframe as "systems engineering" exposure, emphasize cross-functional experience, AGILE methodology |
| **Limited Manufacturing/Design Internship** | No hands-on CAD/production internship experience | Capstone project becomes critical—must present it professionally. Consider Spring 2026 part-time work if possible. |
| **Clearance Not Active** | BAE internship may not have required clearance | Doesn't matter for new grads—companies sponsor clearances. US citizenship is the key requirement (presumably satisfied). |

### 🎯 Actions to Address Gaps
1. **Don't list GPA on resume** unless required—lead with QUEST honors status
2. **Prepare strong capstone presentation** with professional portfolio
3. **Get BAE Systems reference/referral** before graduation
4. **Consider certifications**: Six Sigma Green Belt, CSWA (Certified SolidWorks Associate)—both achievable in weeks

## Market Outlook & Salary Expectations

### Industry Employment Distribution (BLS Data)
- **Manufacturing**: 46% of mechanical engineers (largest sector)
- **Engineering Consulting**: 16%
- **Research & Development**: 6%
- **Government**: 4%
- **Energy/Utilities**: Growing segment

### Fastest Growing Sectors
1. **Renewable Energy**: 15-20% annual growth
2. **Electric Vehicles**: Major transformation underway
3. **Industrial Automation/Robotics**: Reshoring driving demand
4. **Defense/Aerospace**: Steady investment, 9% growth

|--------|-------|-------|
