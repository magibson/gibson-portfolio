## Resume Optimization

### Format Recommendations
- **Length**: 1 page (non-negotiable for new grads)
- **Font**: Clean, professional (Arial, Calibri, Garamond)
- **Margins**: 0.5-0.75 inches
- **No photos, graphics, or colors** (ATS-friendly)

### Recommended Section Order
1. Contact Info (Name, Phone, Email, LinkedIn, Location)
2. Education
3. Technical Skills
4. Experience
5. Projects (Capstone + QUEST)
6. Leadership (Snider, Sigma Phi Epsilon)

### Resume Power Verbs for Engineers
- Designed, Developed, Engineered, Modeled
- Analyzed, Optimized, Validated, Tested
- Led, Managed, Coordinated, Directed
- Improved, Reduced, Increased, Enhanced
- Implemented, Integrated, Automated, Streamlined

### ❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use "Responsibilities include..." (use action verbs)
- Don't include high school achievements
- Don't use "References available upon request"
- Don't include personal interests unless directly relevant

## LinkedIn Profile Strategy

### Profile Completeness Checklist
- [ ] Professional headshot (smile, neutral background)
- [ ] Custom banner image (engineering-related or UMD)
- [ ] Compelling headline (not just "Student")
- [ ] Complete About section
- [ ] All education details
- [ ] All experience with descriptions
- [ ] Skills section (50+ skills)
- [ ] Request 3-5 recommendations

### Headline Formula
**Current Student:**
```
Mechanical Engineering @ UMD | QUEST Honors | Seeking Full-Time 2026
```

**Or more specific:**
```
ME Student | Automotive Design & FEA | QUEST Scholar | BAE Systems Alum
```

### About Section Template (Edit for voice)
```
Mechanical Engineering senior at UMD's QUEST Honors Program, combining 
engineering expertise with business acumen through team-based, real-world 
projects.

My experience spans:
→ Automotive product development (electrochromic sun visor capstone)
→ Defense industry systems (BAE Systems internship)
→ International manufacturing (Japan/Korea study program)
→ Consulting leadership (Chairman, Snider Consulting Group)

Technical toolkit: SolidWorks, FEA/ANSYS, MATLAB, Python, Arduino

I'm seeking full-time mechanical engineering roles starting Summer 2026, 
particularly in automotive, defense/aerospace, or manufacturing. 

Let's connect: [email]
```

### Key Optimization Tips

1. **Use industry keywords** throughout profile:
   - SolidWorks, AutoCAD, CATIA
   - FEA, FEM, CFD
   - DFMEA, FMEA, Six Sigma
   - GD&T, tolerance analysis
   - Product development, design for manufacturing

2. **Turn on "Open to Work"** (visible to recruiters only)

3. **Featured section**: Add capstone project photos/video, QUEST program info

4. **Activity**: Post/share engineering content 1-2x per week

5. **Engage**: Comment thoughtfully on posts from target companies

### Connection Strategy
- Connect with 50+ recruiters from target companies
- Connect with UMD engineering alumni (filter by company)
- Join groups: ASME, SAE International, industry-specific
- Follow all target company pages
