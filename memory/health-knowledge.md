## 2. Nutrition Basics for Mid-20s Active Male

### Macro Guidelines

**Protein Target:** 
- 1.6-2.0g per kg bodyweight for muscle building/maintenance
- For 180lb (82kg) guy: **130-165g protein per day**
- Spread across 4+ meals (30-40g per meal max for absorption)

**Carbs:**
- 40-50% of total calories
- More on training days, slightly less on rest days
- Focus on: rice, potatoes, oats, fruits, vegetables

**Fats:**
- 25-35% of total calories
- ~0.3-0.5g per lb bodyweight
- Sources: olive oil, avocado, nuts, fatty fish

**Rough Daily Targets (active 180lb male, ~2500 cal):**
- Protein: 150g (600 cal)
- Carbs: 275-300g (1100-1200 cal)
- Fat: 75-85g (675-765 cal)

## 3. Exercise Consistency Tips

### Realistic Workout Schedules

**The "3 Days is Enough" Framework:**
- 3x/week full body is plenty for 90% of goals
- Example: Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat
- If you hit 3, anything extra is bonus

**The "Never Miss Twice" Rule:**
- Missed Monday? Fine. Don't miss Wednesday.
- Consistency over perfection

**Mini Workouts Count:**
- 3x 10-minute sessions = same benefits as 30 continuous
- "No time" isn't a reason — 15 minutes works

**Sample Weekly Schedule:**
```
Mon: Full body strength (45-60 min)
Tue: Rest or 20-min walk/cardio
Wed: Full body strength (45-60 min)
Thu: Rest
Fri: Full body strength (45-60 min)
Sat: Something active you enjoy
Sun: Rest
```

### Home vs Gym Tradeoffs

**Home Gym Pros:**
- Zero commute (biggest factor)
- No waiting for equipment
- Work out any time
- One-time equipment cost

**Home Gym Cons:**
- Limited equipment unless you invest
- Easier to get distracted
- Less social motivation

**Gym Pros:**
- Full equipment access
- Separation of space (mental switch)
- Social/community aspect
- Classes available

**Gym Cons:**
- Commute time adds up
- Monthly cost
- Crowded during peak hours

**Verdict:** If consistency is the problem, home gym wins. Remove friction. A pull-up bar, dumbbells, and a bench covers 80% of needs.

**Minimum Home Setup (~$200-400):**
- Adjustable dumbbells or dumbbell set
- Pull-up bar
- Resistance bands
- Yoga mat

### Building Habits That Stick

**Environment Design:**
- Lay out gym clothes night before
- Put gym bag by door
- Keep home equipment visible
- Remove friction between you and working out

**Habit Stacking:**
- "After I pour my morning coffee, I do 10 pushups"
- Attach new habit to existing routine

**Make It Obvious:**
- Schedule workouts as calendar appointments
- Set phone reminder 30 min before
- Tell someone your schedule (accountability)

**Start Stupidly Small:**
- First week: just show up and do 10 min
- Build the habit first, intensity later
- "Just put on gym clothes" can be the goal

**Track It:**
- Use Whoop recovery data to schedule hard days
- Simple check mark in calendar builds momentum
- Don't break the chain

## Weight Loss

*Research compiled for Matt's specific situation: 25yo, desk job, already doing IF, inconsistent gym attendance*

### 1. Intermittent Fasting Optimization

**Best Eating Windows:**
- 16:8 (skip breakfast, eat 12pm-8pm) is the most studied and sustainable
- 18:6 shows slightly more fat loss in studies but harder to stick with
- Matt's current window (likely ~12pm-8pm): solid choice, don't overthink it
- **Timing matters less than consistency** — pick a window and stick to it

**What to Eat During Your Window:**
- Protein first at each meal (helps satiety, preserves muscle)
- Don't compensate with larger portions — IF only works if total calories stay controlled
- Break fast with real food, not sugar — blood sugar spike after fasting causes crash
- Good first meal: protein + fat + fiber (eggs, meat with veggies, Greek yogurt)

**Common IF Mistakes:**
1. **Treating eating window as "free pass"** — IF doesn't override calories in/out
2. **Not enough protein** — muscle loss accelerates in calorie deficit without adequate protein
3. **Drinking calories during fast** — coffee with sugar/cream breaks the fast
4. **Extending fast too aggressively** — 16:8 is plenty; longer isn't necessarily better
5. **Eating too close to bed** — hurts sleep quality, which hurts fat loss

### 2. Gym Strategies for Fat Loss

**The Cardio vs Weights Debate (Research Says):**
- **Resistance training + calorie deficit beats cardio for body composition** (BMJ meta-analysis 2025)
- Lifting preserves muscle mass while losing fat; cardio alone loses both
- Cardio burns more calories *during* workout, but lifting raises resting metabolism
- **Best approach: lift weights 3x/week, add cardio only if needed**
- If doing both: lift first, cardio after (glycogen depleted = more fat burning)

**Efficient Routines for Busy People (45 min or less):**

*Full Body 3x/Week Template (Mon/Wed/Fri):*
```
Day A:
- Squat or Leg Press: 3x8-10
- Bench Press or Push-ups: 3x8-10
- Bent-Over Row: 3x8-10
- Overhead Press: 3x8-10
- Core work: 2x15 (planks, crunches)

Day B:
- Deadlift or Romanian DL: 3x6-8
- Incline Press or Dumbbell Press: 3x8-10
- Lat Pulldown or Pull-ups: 3x8-10
- Lunges or Step-ups: 3x10 each leg
- Core work: 2x15

Alternate A/B each session. Done in 40-45 min.
```

**How Often to Go Realistically:**
- **3x/week is plenty for fat loss + muscle retention**
- Going 5-6x doesn't accelerate fat loss meaningfully
- Missed days matter less than you think — just don't miss twice in a row
- 2x/week still works if that's what you can sustain

**Progressive Overload Basics:**
- Add weight (even 2.5-5 lbs) when you can complete all sets/reps with good form
- If stuck: add 1 rep, then reset weight higher next week
- Track lifts (app like Strong or Hevy) — if not tracking, you're guessing
- Progress isn't linear; weekly fluctuations are normal. Look at monthly trends.

### 3. Calorie Deficit Basics

**Calculating a Reasonable Deficit:**
- Maintenance calories ≈ bodyweight (lbs) × 14-16 (desk job = lower end)
- For 180lb guy: ~2500-2900 maintenance
- **Target deficit: 300-500 cal/day** = 0.5-1 lb/week loss
- Aggressive deficits (1000+) backfire — muscle loss, metabolic slowdown, binge cycles

**Quick Math:**
```
Maintenance: ~2500 cal (180lb, sedentary + 3x gym)
Fat loss target: 2000-2200 cal/day
Expected loss: ~0.5-1 lb/week
```

**Protein Importance While Cutting:**
- Research shows **1.6-2.4g per kg bodyweight** preserves muscle during deficit
- For 180lb (82kg): **130-200g protein daily**
- Higher protein = less muscle loss = better body composition at end of cut
- Also: protein is most satiating macro — helps with hunger
- 2.4g/kg showed better results than 1.2g/kg in deficit study (Longland 2016)

**Why Crash Diets Fail:**
1. **Muscle loss** — aggressive deficits burn muscle, not just fat
2. **Metabolic adaptation** — body reduces energy expenditure to match intake
3. **Hunger hormones spike** — ghrelin (hunger) increases, leptin (satiety) decreases
4. **Willpower depletes** — extreme restriction leads to binge/restrict cycles
5. **Unsustainable** — 8 weeks of suffering followed by regaining it all

**Bottom line:** Moderate deficit (300-500) + high protein + lifting = lose fat, keep muscle, actually sustain it.

### 4. Practical Tips

**Tracking Methods That Aren't Obsessive:**
- **Don't weigh everything forever** — track strictly for 2-4 weeks to learn portions, then estimate
- Use hand-based portions: palm = protein serving, fist = carbs/veggies, thumb = fats
- Weigh yourself daily BUT only look at weekly average (daily fluctuates ±2-4 lbs from water)
- Apps: MacroFactor adapts to your actual metabolism; Cronometer is accurate but tedious
- Focus on protein target first, let other macros fall where they may

**Dealing with Desk Job Metabolism:**
- NEAT (non-exercise activity) matters more than you think — desk workers burn 300-500 fewer cal/day
- Walk during calls, take stairs, stand desk if available
- "Movement snacks": 5 min walk every hour adds up to 40 min/day
- Park far, get off a stop early — small stuff compounds
- After lunch: 10-15 min walk helps insulin sensitivity and prevents afternoon crash

**Sleep and Stress Impact on Weight:**
- **Sleep deprivation tanks fat loss** — study showed 55% less fat loss with same calories when sleep-deprived
- Poor sleep increases ghrelin (hunger) by 28% and decreases leptin (satiety) by 18%
- Cortisol (stress hormone) promotes fat storage, especially visceral (belly) fat
- Sleep-deprived people lose more muscle and store more fat than rested people
- **Minimum 7 hours, ideally 7.5-8** — this isn't optional for fat loss

**Cortisol/Stress Management:**
- Chronically elevated cortisol = body holds onto fat
- High stress + calorie deficit = recipe for muscle loss + fat retention
- Don't add aggressive training on top of high work stress
- Whoop recovery score is useful here — red days = back off intensity
