## 1. How to Access WARN Notices

### New Jersey Department of Labor

**Primary URL:** https://www.nj.gov/labor/employer-services/warn/

**Direct Links to WARN Archives:**
- **2026 notices:** https://www.nj.gov/labor/assets/PDFs/WARN/2026_WARN_Notice_Archive.pdf
- **2025 notices:** https://www.nj.gov/labor/assets/PDFs/WARN/2025_WARN_Notice_Archive.pdf
- **Historical Excel file (2004-2025):** Available on the main WARN page

**Update frequency:** Monthly PDFs, typically updated as new notices are filed

**Information included:**
- Company name
- City/Location
- Month posted
- Effective date of layoff
- Number of workforce affected

### Pennsylvania Department of Labor & Industry

**Primary URL:** https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dli/programs-services/workforce-development-home/warn-requirements/warn-notices

**Also accessible at:** https://www.dli.pa.gov/Individuals/Workforce-Development/warn/Pages/default.aspx

**Update frequency:** Updated as notices are filed, organized by year and month

**Information included:**
- Company name
- Full address
- County
- Number affected
- Effective date (sometimes a range)
- Closure vs. Layoff designation

### Third-Party Aggregators

**WARNTracker.com** - https://www.warntracker.com/
- Aggregates WARN data from all states
- Searchable database going back to 1988
- 37,555+ companies tracked
- Offers data export options (paid)
- Good for seeing national trends

**Layoffs.fyi** - https://layoffs.fyi/
- Tech-focused layoff tracker
- Good for tracking tech companies specifically

## 2. Prospecting Strategy

### Timing: When to Reach Out

**The WARN Act requires 60 days advance notice**, so you have a window:

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Day 1-14 after notice posted** | Research the company, identify potential contacts |
| **Day 15-30** | Initial educational outreach (NOT salesy) |
| **Day 30-45** | Follow-up with specific value |
| **Day 45-60** | Decision time - offer consultation |
| **Post-layoff (Day 60+)** | People now have their severance packages in hand |

**Sweet spot:** 2-4 weeks before the effective layoff date. People know it's coming, they're anxious but haven't made decisions yet.

**Avoid:** Reaching out the day the notice is posted (too early, feels predatory) or after they've already been terminated (too late, they've likely made decisions).

### How to Find Affected Employees

**LinkedIn Strategies:**

1. **Search by company name + "open to work"**
   - Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator for best results
   - Filter by location (NJ/PA)
   - Look for the green "Open to Work" frame on profiles

2. **Boolean search examples:**
   ```
   "Company Name" AND ("open to work" OR "seeking" OR "looking for opportunities")
   ```

3. **Current/Past Company filter:**
   - Search people who list the company as current employer
   - Filter by NJ/PA locations
   - Cross-reference with WARN timeline

4. **Monitor company pages:**
   - Follow companies that have filed WARN notices
   - Watch for employees posting about transitions

5. **LinkedIn hashtags:**
   - #OpenToWork
   - #LayOff
   - #CareerTransition

**Other methods:**
- Local Facebook groups (community/job seeker groups)
- Nextdoor in affected areas
- Local news coverage of layoffs (often quotes employees)

### Messaging: Helpful, Not Predatory

**DON'T:**
- Lead with your services
- Use fear-based language
- Cold call aggressively
- Mass-blast identical messages
- Mention you saw them on a WARN list

**DO:**
- Lead with value and education
- Be empathetic and human
- Offer free resources first
- Make it easy to ignore if they're not interested
- Position yourself as a resource, not a salesperson

**Sample outreach messages:**

**Educational/Soft approach (LinkedIn):**
> "Hi [Name] - I noticed you're going through a career transition. I work with a lot of folks navigating similar changes, and I put together a quick checklist of financial decisions that come up in the first 60 days. No strings attached if you'd like a copy. Wishing you the best in your search."

**Direct but empathetic approach:**
> "Hi [Name], I saw that [Company] announced some workforce changes. I specialize in helping people in transition make smart decisions about their 401k, severance, and benefits - the stuff that often gets rushed when there's a deadline. Happy to answer any questions you might have, no obligation."

**Content-first approach:**
> Share a LinkedIn post titled "5 Financial Decisions You Need to Make Within 60 Days of a Layoff" - then the people who engage are self-selecting as interested.

### Services These People Actually Need

**1. 401(k) Rollover Decisions**
- Roll to IRA vs. leave in employer plan vs. cash out
- Tax implications of each option
- Investment selection in new account
- Roth conversion opportunities (if income will be lower this year)

**2. Severance Package Analysis**
- Lump sum vs. structured payments (if offered)
- Tax withholding optimization
- Timing of payments relative to calendar year
- Impact on unemployment benefits

**3. Emergency Fund Planning**
- How long will severance + savings last?
- Creating a realistic "runway" calculation
- Where to park emergency savings
- Priority of expenses during transition

**4. Health Insurance: COBRA vs. ACA Marketplace**
- COBRA is expensive (you pay full premium + 2% admin fee)
- ACA Marketplace may offer subsidies based on income
- Job loss is a "qualifying life event" = 60-day special enrollment
- Help them compare actual costs side-by-side

**5. Career Transition Financial Planning**
- Adjusting budget for reduced/no income
- Should they tap retirement accounts? (Usually no)
- Side income tax implications
- Stock option deadline decisions (often 90 days post-termination)

**6. Debt Management**
- Should they pay off debt with severance?
- Mortgage forbearance options
- Student loan deferment/IBR recalculation

## 3. Compliance Considerations

### FINRA/SEC Rules

There's no specific FINRA rule prohibiting outreach to laid-off employees. However:

- **Suitability:** Any recommendations must be suitable for the client's situation
- **Fair Dealing:** Communications must be fair, balanced, and not misleading
- **Supervision:** Your firm's compliance policies may have specific rules about cold outreach

### Best Practices for Staying Compliant

1. **Document everything** - Keep records of all outreach and communications
2. **Don't make promises** - Avoid guarantees about returns or outcomes
3. **Disclose conflicts** - Be clear about how you're compensated
4. **Firm approval** - Get approval for any marketing materials or templates
5. **Do Not Call list** - If phone calling, check the national registry

### Keeping It Tasteful

**The ethics test:** Would you be comfortable if this message appeared in a news story about "financial advisors preying on laid-off workers"?

**Good indicators:**
- You're offering something of value before asking for anything
- The person can easily ignore you with no follow-up harassment
- Your message focuses on their needs, not your services
- You're genuinely trying to help, with business as a secondary goal

**Red flags:**
- Buying lists of terminated employees
- Aggressive follow-up after no response
- Fear-mongering language
- Exaggerating urgency or implying they'll make mistakes without you

## 4. Automation Ideas

### Monitoring WARN Filings

**The Challenge:**
- NJ uses PDFs (not machine-readable friendly)
- PA uses a web page that changes structure
- No official RSS feeds from either state

**Potential Solutions:**

**Option 1: Manual weekly check (simplest)**
- Set a calendar reminder every Monday
- Check both state pages
- Log new filings in a spreadsheet
- 15-minute weekly investment

**Option 2: PDF monitoring service**
- Services like Visualping or Distill.io can monitor web pages/PDFs for changes
- Set up alerts for NJ and PA WARN pages
- Get notified when new content appears
- ~$5-15/month

**Option 3: Custom scraping solution**
- Python script using requests + BeautifulSoup (for PA)
- PDF parsing library for NJ files
- Store in database, send email alerts
- Requires technical skills or developer help
- Could check daily and email new filings

**Option 4: Use WARNTracker.com**
- They do the aggregation work
- Subscribe to their newsletter
- Filter for NJ/PA

## 5. Quick Reference Checklist

### Weekly WARN Monitoring Routine

- [ ] Check NJ WARN PDF (Monday)
- [ ] Check PA WARN page (Monday)
- [ ] Log new filings with company, location, # affected, date
- [ ] Research companies on LinkedIn
- [ ] Identify potential contacts
- [ ] Add to outreach queue with appropriate timing

### Outreach Checklist

- [ ] Wait appropriate time (not immediate)
- [ ] Personalize message (don't mass blast)
- [ ] Lead with value, not pitch
- [ ] Offer free resource or consultation
- [ ] Single follow-up max if no response
- [ ] Track in CRM

### Client Meeting Agenda (New Layoff Client)

1. **Review severance package** - What did they get?
2. **401(k) options** - Leave, roll over, or other?
3. **Timeline** - When do they need to decide?
4. **Health insurance** - COBRA deadline and ACA options
5. **Cash flow** - How long can current savings last?
6. **Emergency moves** - Anything urgent we need to address?
