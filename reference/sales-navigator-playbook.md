## Profile Optimization

Before prospecting, ensure your profile builds trust at first glance.

### Profile Photo
- High-resolution (450×450px minimum)
- Professional attire matching client expectations
- Simple or blurred background
- Warm, genuine expression

### Custom Header Image (1128×191px)
- Include your photo, company logo, and a CTA
- Example CTA: "Schedule a free financial review" or "Download my retirement planning guide"
- Tools: Canva.com or Fiverr for professional design

### Headline Formula
```
[Title] | Helping [Target Market] [Achieve Outcome]
```

**Examples for financial advisors:**
- "Financial Advisor | Helping Young Professionals Build Generational Wealth"
- "Wealth Manager at New York Life | Retirement Planning for Business Owners"
- "CFP® | Guiding Families Toward Financial Independence"

### Summary Structure
1. **Opening hook** - Question or statement that resonates with target clients
2. **Who you help** - Be specific about your ideal client
3. **How you help** - Your approach and services
4. **Why you're different** - Your values, certifications, experience
5. **Call to action** - Invite them to connect or schedule a call

### Featured Section
Pin high-value content:
- Free resources (eBooks, guides, checklists)
- Client testimonials (with proper disclosures)
- Webinar recordings or podcasts
- Links to your website or booking page

## Search Filters & Boolean Queries

### Essential Filters for Financial Advisor Prospecting

#### Demographics
- **Geography:** Your service area (e.g., "Greater New York Area")
- **Seniority Level:** Director, VP, C-Suite, Owner
- **Company Size:** 50-200, 200-500, 500-1000 employees
- **Industry:** Target industries where you have expertise

#### Spotlights (Trigger Filters)
- **Changed jobs in past 90 days** - New role = new financial needs
- **Posted on LinkedIn in past 30 days** - Active users more likely to respond
- **Mentioned in the news** - Good conversation opener
- **Shared connections** - Warm introduction paths

### Boolean Search Strings for Financial Advisors

**High-Net-Worth Professionals:**
```
("Vice President" OR "VP" OR "Director" OR "C-Suite" OR "CEO" OR "CFO" OR "Partner") 
AND NOT ("Assistant" OR "Intern" OR "Entry Level")
```

**Business Owners & Executives:**
```
("Founder" OR "Owner" OR "CEO" OR "President" OR "Managing Partner") 
AND ("tech" OR "healthcare" OR "real estate" OR "consulting")
```

**Retirement Planning Prospects (50+):**
```
("Director" OR "VP" OR "Senior") 
AND ("25+ years experience" OR "30+ years")
```

**Young Professionals Building Wealth:**
```
("Manager" OR "Senior" OR "Lead") 
AND ("promoted" OR "new role") 
AND (NOT "student" OR "intern")
```

**Pre-Retirees with Equity Compensation:**
```
("VP" OR "Director" OR "Senior Manager") 
AND ("stock options" OR "RSU" OR "equity")
```

**Wealth Manager Targets (High-Net-Worth):**
```
("Wealth Manager" OR "Private Banker") 
AND ("high net worth" OR "HNWI" OR "affluent clients") 
AND ("estate planning" OR "wealth transfer") 
AND (NOT "insurance agent")
```

### Title Filter Examples
For Current Job Title filter, use:
- `CFO OR "Chief Financial Officer" OR "Finance Director"`
- `"Business Owner" OR "Founder" OR "CEO" OR "President"`
- `"VP Sales" OR "VP Marketing" OR "VP Operations"`

### Saving Searches
1. Run your search with desired filters
2. Click "Save Search" in top right
3. Name it descriptively (e.g., "NYC Business Owners - Recent Job Change")
4. Set alert frequency (daily or weekly)

## Life Event Triggers

Life events create natural opportunities for financial conversations. Use Sales Navigator Spotlights and Alerts to identify these moments.

### High-Value Trigger Events

| Trigger | Why It Matters | How to Find |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Job change** | New income, benefits decisions, 401k rollover | "Changed jobs in past 90 days" filter |
| **Promotion** | Increased income, stock compensation | Monitor saved leads for role changes |
| **New executive role** | Complex compensation, planning needs | Search new VPs, Directors, C-Suite |
| **Company IPO/acquisition** | Liquidity event, stock decisions | News alerts, funding event filter |
| **Posted about life event** | Marriage, baby, home purchase | Content scanning in feed |
| **Company growth** | Hiring signals = success, liquidity | "Headcount growth" filter |
| **Approaching retirement** | 50s/60s with senior titles | Years of experience + seniority |
| **Business sale/exit** | Major wealth event | Monitor entrepreneurs, founders |

### Outreach Timing by Trigger

**Job Change (Best: Days 30-90)**
- First 30 days: Too busy onboarding
- Days 30-90: Settling in, making decisions about benefits/401k rollover
- Message angle: "Congrats on the new role! Many executives I work with take the first 90 days to review their financial picture..."

**Promotion**
- Reach out within 1-2 weeks
- Message angle: "Saw your promotion—well deserved! Increased income often triggers questions about tax optimization..."

**Funding/IPO**
- Reach out 1-4 weeks after announcement
- Message angle: "Congrats on the [Series B/IPO]! Executives at this stage often benefit from..."

## InMail Strategies

### Key Statistics
- Average InMail response rate: **18-25%** (vs 1-3% for cold email)
- Messages under 400 characters get **22% higher response**
- Best day: **Tuesday** morning
- New job holders are **3x more likely** to respond

### InMail Best Practices

1. **Keep it short** - Under 400 characters ideal
2. **Personalize the first line** - Reference their profile, post, or news
3. **Lead with value** - What's in it for them?
4. **One clear CTA** - Single ask, easy to answer
5. **Don't pitch immediately** - Offer insight, start conversation

### InMail Templates

**Template 1: Job Change Trigger**
```
Hi [First Name],

Saw you recently joined [Company] as [Title] – congrats! 

Many executives I work with use a role change as a moment to 
review their 401k rollover options and tax planning. If that's 
on your radar, happy to share a quick framework that's helped 
others in similar transitions.

No pressure either way – just wanted to offer if helpful.

[Your name]
```

**Template 2: Mutual Connection Reference**
```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed we're both connected to [Mutual Connection Name]. 

I specialize in helping [target: e.g., "tech executives"] 
navigate [pain point: e.g., "equity compensation decisions"], 
and thought it might be worth a quick intro.

Would you be open to a 15-min call to see if there's a fit?

[Your name]
```

**Template 3: Content Engagement**
```
Hi [First Name],

Really enjoyed your post on [topic]. Your point about [specific insight] 
resonated—I see similar patterns with the executives I advise.

I put together a brief guide on [related topic] that might add to 
your thinking. Happy to share if you're interested.

[Your name]
```

**Template 4: News/Achievement**
```
Hi [First Name],

Congrats on [achievement/news mention]! Impressive work.

I work with [target audience] who've been through similar 
milestones, and there are often some unique planning considerations 
that come up at this stage.

If you'd ever like an outside perspective on the financial side, 
I'd be happy to chat. No pitch, just insight.

[Your name]
```

### Subject Line Best Practices
- Keep under 6 words
- Be specific, not generic
- Reference something personal
- Examples:
  - "Quick question about [Company name]"
  - "Congrats on the promotion"
  - "Saw your post on [topic]"
  - "[Mutual connection] suggested we connect"

**What it does:** Waterfall enrichment from 75+ data sources, AI-powered research

**Best for:**
- Enriching exported Sales Navigator leads with emails, phone numbers
- Finding company news, funding data, tech stack
- Personalizing outreach at scale with AI

**How to use with Sales Navigator:**
1. Export leads from Sales Navigator (via Evaboot or similar)
2. Import CSV to Clay
3. Run enrichment (email, phone, company data)
4. Use Clay's AI to research each prospect
5. Export to your outreach tool or CRM

**Cost:** Starts ~$149/mo for serious users

**X Chatter (Feb 2026):** "Clay dominates LinkedIn enrichment—waterfall approach 2-3x more phone numbers than single-source tools"

## Compliance Considerations

### LinkedIn Terms of Service

**What's NOT Allowed:**
- Automated connection requests or messages
- Scraping data at scale
- Using bots to interact with profiles
- Creating fake profiles
- Sending spam or bulk unsolicited messages

**What LinkedIn May Do:**
- Restrict your account temporarily
- Permanently ban repeat offenders
- Remove connections made through automation

**Safe Practices:**
- Keep outreach manual and personal
- Stay within LinkedIn's daily limits (50-100 connection requests)
- Use automation tools for data export only, not messaging
- Personalize every message

---

### FINRA Compliance for Financial Advisors

As a registered rep at New York Life, your LinkedIn activity is subject to FINRA Rule 2210 (Communications with the Public).

**Key Requirements:**

1. **Archiving (Rule 2210)**
   - All business communications must be retained for 3+ years
   - This includes LinkedIn posts, comments, and messages
   - Use New York Life's approved archiving solution

2. **Principal Approval**
   - Static content (posts, profile updates) must be pre-approved
   - Interactive content (comments, replies) must be monitored
   - Recommendations for specific products require pre-approval or approved templates

3. **Content Standards**
   - Must be balanced and complete
   - No false, misleading, or exaggerated claims
   - No performance predictions or projections
   - Include proper disclosures on promotional content

4. **Third-Party Content**
   - Sharing/liking content = "adopting" it
   - You're responsible for any content you endorse
   - Review third-party sources before sharing

5. **Testimonials**
   - Client testimonials require disclosures
   - Must clarify if compensated
   - Cannot imply future performance

---

### Practical Compliance Tips

**Do:**
- Run LinkedIn posts through your compliance department
- Keep copies of all outreach messages
- Use approved templates when possible
- Keep educational content general, not product-specific
- Include your FINRA disclosures where required

**Don't:**
- Promise specific returns or outcomes
- Share client testimonials without proper disclosure
- Use automation tools for mass outreach
- Make product recommendations in cold outreach
- Post about specific investment products without approval

**When in Doubt:**
- Ask your Chief Compliance Officer
- Stick to educational, value-added content
- Keep initial outreach about connecting, not selling

## Weekly Workflow

### Time Investment: 2-3 hours/week

Here's a structured weekly cadence for Sales Navigator prospecting.

---

### Monday: Research & List Building (45 min)

**Morning (30 min):**
1. Check Sales Navigator homepage for alerts
2. Review job changes, news mentions, content posts
3. Flag hot prospects for immediate outreach

**Afternoon (15 min):**
1. Run saved searches for new prospects
2. Export 20-30 new leads to your CRM/Notion
3. Request Clawdbot to research and enrich

---

### Tuesday: Outreach Day (45 min)

*Best day for InMails - highest response rates*

**Tasks:**
1. Review Clawdbot's research on this week's leads
2. Send 10-15 personalized InMails
3. Send 10-15 connection requests with notes
4. Respond to any replies from previous outreach

**InMail targets:**
- 5 job-change prospects
- 5 high-value executives
- 5 from saved searches

---

### Wednesday: Engagement & Content (30 min)

**Tasks:**
1. Engage with prospects' content (like, comment meaningfully)
2. Post your own content (if approved)
3. Check for new connection acceptances
4. Send welcome messages to new connections

**Content Ideas:**
- Market commentary (general, not specific advice)
- Educational tips (retirement planning basics, etc.)
- Industry news and insights
- Personal/professional story

---

### Thursday: Follow-Up (30 min)

**Tasks:**
1. Follow up on InMails sent last Tuesday (if no response)
2. Move replied leads to "Call Scheduled" or next stage
3. Update CRM/Notion with new statuses
4. Identify dead leads to archive

**Follow-up message framework:**
```
Hi [Name], just wanted to float this back up in case it got buried. 
No pressure—happy to connect whenever it makes sense. [Your name]
```

---

### Friday: Review & Plan (30 min)

**Tasks:**
1. Review the week's metrics (see tracking section)
2. Note what's working, what's not
3. Identify prospects for next week's outreach
4. Request Clawdbot to prep next week's research

**Weekly Questions:**
- How many InMails sent? Response rate?
- How many connections requested? Accepted?
- How many calls scheduled?
- Which triggers/approaches worked best?
